The Crisis in Syria


                     

Learn about the lives of the innocent civilians who had no choice but to flee their homes in search of a new and safe life.

Quick Facts:
More than 11 million people have left their home. Of this, 4 million civilians have left Syria.

Half of these are children.

Most of the Syrian refugees remain in the Middle East, Turkey, Lebanon, Jordan, Iraq, and Egypt.

The children affected by the conflict are at risk of becoming malnourished, abused, or exploited. Millions have been forced to quit school.


A refugee is defined as any person who has been forced to leave their country in order to escape war, persecution, or natural disaster. This could be anyone ranging from a doctor, school teacher, a housewife and children. The people fleeing syria are doing so because they are scared for their lives.



Many surrounding countries of Syria are beginning to close their borders because the intake of Syrian refugees is becoming too much to handle. However, many countries provide as much relief as they can for the refugees. Germany will expect to resettle 800,000 Syrian refugees this year, with aid of $9.6 billion. France's President said that they would settle 24,000 migrants. Even Australia plans to resettle 12,000 refugees from Syria and will donate $44 million to provide aid.
