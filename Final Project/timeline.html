The Crisis in Syria


                     

Take a look at what major events occured that set off the Syrian War.

1970 November New President Hafez Al-Assad overthrows president Nur al-Din al-Atasi.
1973 September Assad drops constitutional requirement stating "president must be a Muslim". Fear broke out that Assad is heading an athiest regime fueling many riots. Also leads to the creation of the Muslim brotherhood.
1982 February President Hafez Al-Assad suppresses Muslim Brother and kills tens of thousands of people. Known as the 1982 Hama massacre.
2000 June Assad dies and succeeded by Bashar, his second son.
2011 March Amongst many uprisings in arab countries, peaceful protests are held in many parts of Syria. They quickly become deadly when the military intervenes killing 100's of people.
2011 May Bashar Assad vows to kill all the 'terrorists'. The protestors calls for the downfall of Assad's regime. Army tanks enter cities Deraa, Banyas, Homs, and Damascus.
2011 November Arab League votes to suspend Syria, accusing it of failing to implement an Arab peace plan, and imposes sanctions.
2011 December Twin suicide bombs outside security buildings in Damascus kills 44. Sparks the first series of large blasts.
2012 April Syria promises to comply with a UN-brockered ceasefire but with a condition that the regime still has a right to defend itself against 'terrorists' behind the uprising.
2012 May Massacre in Houla village in Homs leave more than 100 killed. UN Human Rights Council releases a report accusing Assad's forces of war crimes during bloodbath.
2012 July Syria threatens to use chemical weapons if any sort of foreign attack were to happen.
2012 August Obama says US will reconsider its opposition to involvment in Syria if Assad's regime deploys chemical or bio weapons.
2013 January Assad ignores demands to step down and blames "murderous criminals" for violence in Syria. Won't stop "as long as there is one terrorist left".
2013 June Amongst the European ending its embargo on sending weapons for Syrian rebels. Obama also authorises sending weapons to Syrian rebels after finding out Assad's Government used chemical weapons.
2014 February A second round of Geneva talks is held.
2014 June Establishment of a new "caliphate" was announced by Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant with Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi named as caliph.
2014 September US and Arab countries launch air strikes against Islamic State.
2015 Decemeber Britain joins US-led bombing raids against IS in wake of Paris suicide bombing attacks. Syrian army allows rebels to evacuate remaining area of Homs, returning Syria's third largest city to government control in 4 years.
